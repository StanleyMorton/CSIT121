Spam musubi Recipe

Erica Walker

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of cooked and seasoned sushi rice
  • 8 sheets of nori (seaweed)
  • 8 slices of Spam
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, sugar, and mirin. Heat over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  2. Cut each slice of Spam into 4 pieces. In a skillet, cook the Spam until crispy on both sides.
  3. Place a piece of plastic wrap on a sushi rolling mat. Lay a sheet of nori, shiny side down, on the plastic wrap.
  4. Wet your hands and grab a handful of sushi rice. Press the rice evenly onto the nori, leaving about 1 inch of nori at the top uncovered.
  5. Place a piece of crispy Spam in the center of the rice, then drizzle some of the soy sauce mixture over it.
  6. Roll the musubi tightly, using the sushi rolling mat. Moisten the exposed nori to seal the roll.
  7. Repeat for the remaining ingredients.
  8. Slice each musubi into bite-sized pieces and sprinkle with sesame seeds (if desired).

Musubi is my favorite recipe because it combines the delicious flavors of crispy Spam, sweet and savory sauce, and perfectly seasoned sushi rice. It's a taste of Hawaii that I can't remind me of the times I was a kid on living on the island of Oahu.
